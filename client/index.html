Today, we’re launching the New York Magazine cover store, where you can buy some of the iconic covers that the magazine has created over its 53-year history. We’re starting with about 50 covers from our archives, from our very first issue from April 1968 to this year’s Fall Preview showcasing the cast of Succession.

There are some other recent favorites, like the New Yawk merch monster from our Spring Fashion issue and our “Reasons to Love New York” cover where we paid tribute to all the places that closed since the pandemic began. You’ll find some classic Milton Glaser illustrations, including “A Gentile’s Guide to Jewish Food” from July 1968 and “Why Everybody’s Talking About Gossip” from May 1976.

We’ve also included some covers and designs you’ve never been able to purchase before, like the one where Bert Stern recreated Marilyn Monroe’s last (nude) photo shoot with Lindsay Lohan.
